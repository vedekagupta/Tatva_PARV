Division of Scriptures in Historical Context

Our ancient scriptures, such as the Vedas, Puranas, and Upanishads, are divided into Shruti and Smriti categories. These divisions reflect the oral traditions and written records that shaped Indian philosophy and history.

Shruti

Shruti refers to the body of sacred texts that are considered to be heard and transmitted verbally from generation to generation. This includes the Vedas and the Upanishads.

Smriti

Smriti encompasses a vast range of texts including the Puranas, Itihasas, Dharma Shastras, and more, which are remembered and written down by sages.

Vedas

The Vedas are the oldest and most authoritative scriptures of Hinduism, consisting of Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, and Atharvaveda.

Puranas

The Puranas are ancient Hindu texts that narrate the history of the universe from creation to destruction, genealogies of kings, heroes, sages, and deities.

Kings and Dynasties

King Ashoka - Maurya Dynasty

King Ashoka, one of the greatest emperors of India, ruled the Maurya Dynasty. He is known for his role in spreading Buddhism and his edicts inscribed on rocks and pillars across the Indian subcontinent.

King Chandragupta - Gupta Dynasty

Chandragupta I was a pivotal figure in Indian history, founding the Gupta Empire, which ushered in a golden age of cultural and scientific achievements.

King Harsha - Vardhana Dynasty

King Harsha ruled Northern India during the early 7th century and was known for his administrative efficiency and the promotion of arts and culture.
